HOW IT WORKS

Syndir a thought experiment on social networking. Psychological assements on users, performed by companies such as Facebook and Cambridge Analytica in secret, have become a highly controversial subject. If these privacy-violating metrics are a natural byproduct of providing psychological samples online, is there a way we can harness them for some good to use to our advantage? Syndir uses a basic sentiment-analysis wordlist to match users on their pyschological inclinations.

Sentiment analysis (also known as opinion mining or emotion AI) refers to the use of natural language processing, text analysis, computational linguistics, and biometrics to systematically identify, extract, quantify, and study affective states and subjective information. Sentiment analysis is widely applied to voice of the customer materials such as reviews and survey responses, online and social media, and healthcare materials for applications that range from marketing to customer service to clinical medicine.

In layman's terms, sentiment analysis is the process of scoring a text sample to gague determining the tone of a text sample by analyzing online pieces of writing to determine the emotional content they carry. It is used to gain an understanding of the the attitudes and opinions expressed within an online mention. It is used to find the author's position towards an issue; it can be used gauge public opinion on policy announcements, brand messaging, stock market activity, and other complex tasks.

AFINN numerically scores pieces of words as relatively (+)positive, or (-)negative using a predefined list of words and their scores. Words absent from the list score 0. Here is an more rigorous explanation of the underlying theory. Implemented with the npm sentiment package.
